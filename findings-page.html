Which Genre Do Zennials Like?

Why ask?

Megurine Luka is an aspiring music artist. And an artist needs an audience to be successful. A good way to gain audience is to produce works according to their taste. Which genre should Megurine focus on?

Let’s help her!

First, we should obtain a sample data (“18,393 Pitchfork Reviews”) from Kaggle. We then clean the data as needed by filling up the null values. Next, we select the records within the year 1995 to 2013. That is supposed to be the time frame of Zennials before the Gen Alpha came in the picture. We then take the average scores of the records. Then we select subsets from the subset. Each sub-subset will be records for each genre. Each sub-subset will be assessed for their average scores. After processing the subset, we found out that the records have an average of 6.951037; the chart is skewed to left as well.

It turns out that the genre global has the highest average among the sub-subsets, and it is also above the average score of the Zennial records.

Should Megurine focus on the global genre?

According to this dataset, the works with global as their genre will get good ratings. But let’s first have a look outside of the dataset. Global music is also known as world music. This is because this genre hosts songs from the outside of the occidental region; it hosts songs from the outside world. By songs from outside of the world, this could mean any style as long as it is not from the occidental region. Examples of the styles include Reggae and Salsa. With globalization connecting the world, cultures are travelling faster and more frequently. Global songs often carry a bit of their culture which the occidental region finds to be unique, fresh, and interesting. This gives the global songs a considerable appeal [1]. The background story matches with the results from the dataset. Thus, we can most likely assume that this genre does get good feedbacks and that Megurine can work on this genre.
